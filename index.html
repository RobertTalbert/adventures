TL;DR

We ran a fully online version of MTH 201 with no synchronous meetings, done with 20 students over Spring and Summer 2015.

It was an awesome challenge to design and teach.

For most students it was a great learning experience both about math and about personal management skills.

Lots of challenges and issues to address for SS2016. But the potential to serve students is great and GVSU Math can occupy a unique niche in online learning.

Exam problems + miniprojects + specs grading

16 Problems, 6 designated as CORE. (link to samples)

8 Miniprojects, 4 designated as CORE.

All graded Mastery/Progressing/Novice. No points/partial credit.

4 Midterm Exams, each cumulative. Any exam problem can be refreshed and retaken at a later exam, including the final exam.

Miniprojects can be revised and resubmitted.

Grade system (basic)

Two reasons I ditched standard grading for specs grading in this class

Harder/more authentic problems + lots of revision: Deters academic dishonesty

Focuses conversation on quality and standards rather than grades and numbers

Post-mortem

Final grades

Exit interview patterns

Most students highly satisfied with the course, rated the experience as superior to any past online course and many F2F courses.

All students indicated they would take another GVSU mathematics course online in the future, or would have in the past had one been offered.

What piece of advice would you offer to future online GVSU math students? Manage your time carefully and be prepared to work harder than F2F.

Three things I learned about online teaching and learning

It's not for the lacksadaiscal -- plan and prepare relentlessly

Teaching and learning are about relationships -- even online

Technology is only as good as its ability to enable and foster relationships

Three things I liked about the experience

Flipped learning and use of technology are given, need no justification

Flexibility with schedule and location

Specs grading meshes well with the "flexibly modular" principle

Three things I didn't expect

How much I would crave human contact

How badly some students would underestimate time and scheduling

How hard it is to maintain academic integrity in an online setting

Three things I will change in SS2015

MORE VIDEO PRESENTATIONS
